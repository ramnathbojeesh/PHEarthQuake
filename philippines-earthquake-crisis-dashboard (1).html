Strategic recommendations

Actions for communications, government liaison, and crisis monitoring teams

01 · Urgent
Accelerate school safety communications
With 3,500 schools still closed and a national debate emerging around DepEd's building safety standards, government communications teams should proactively publish structural assessment timelines and a return-to-school roadmap. The "first day of school" framing will continue to dominate global coverage — proactive transparency reduces reputational risk for both DepEd and the Marcos administration.
02 · Urgent
Address connectivity gaps in remote barangays
While NGCP restored transmission, barangay-level distribution gaps and damaged road access continue to isolate communities. DSWD and DOH need satellite comms and airlift logistics to reach areas cut off by landslides. Unserved populations represent both a humanitarian risk and a media risk — "abandoned communities" narratives are already emerging in regional Filipino media.
03 · Amplify
Highlight NGCP power restoration as a government win
Restoring power to all hospitals and key infrastructure within 30 hours is a significant crisis response achievement. Government communicators should amplify this milestone through official channels and social media — it provides a positive counter-narrative to the dominant crisis framing and demonstrates institutional competence during the emergency.
04 · Monitor
Monitor school collapse narrative for escalation
The "DepEd urged to demolish unsafe buildings" coverage carries long-tail reputational risk. If a school building collapses during an aftershock while students are present, the story escalates immediately to a national accountability crisis. Track DepEd's structural assessment announcements daily and pre-brief spokespeople for this scenario.
05 · Watch
GenSan Airport: track logistics impact on aid delivery
Major damage to General Santos International Airport is constraining the speed of incoming aid, international volunteers, and reconstruction materials. Monitor CAAP's repair timeline. If the airport remains closed beyond 2 weeks, expect critical reporting on relief bottlenecks — coordinate with DPWH and CAAP on a public-facing repair schedule.
06 · Opportunity
Leverage international solidarity for long-term rebuild narrative
Aid from Japan, Australia, and UN agencies presents an opportunity to frame this as a story of international solidarity. Government and media communications teams should publish regular "recovery scorecards" — tracking homes rebuilt, schools reopened, and families housed — converting crisis coverage into long-term resilience storytelling as the news cycle evolves.
